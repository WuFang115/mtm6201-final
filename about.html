From Street to Sofa: Willow’s Journey

Willow was found shivering beneath a highway overpass on a -20 °C January night, her paw pads raw from road salt and an old collar embedded in her neck. Volunteers rushed her to our partner clinic for hypothermia treatment and emergency surgery, then nursed her through two fragile weeks of IV fluids, antibiotics, and a donated heated kennel. When the stitches healed, she moved into a foster home where she met soft blankets, squeaky toys, and—after much coaxing—her first couch nap. Gradually, weekly physiotherapy loosened stiff muscles, and peanut-butter Kongs coaxed out a full-body tail-wag no one had seen before.

Eight weeks later, Willow trotted out in a rainbow “Second Chances” bandana to meet her adopters—a family who now takes her hiking in Gatineau Park every weekend. She greets neighbours like old friends, insists on sleeping halfway across her people’s pillows, and reminds us daily that rescued dogs never forget what it feels like to be safe.
